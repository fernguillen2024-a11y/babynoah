The Idea

Every member puts in $50 a month. That money doesn’t sit in a drawer — it gets put to work.

We flip cars, sell food and drinks at shows, and take on whatever money-making ideas the group approves. The profits get split between every member. The bigger we grow the pot, the more everyone earns. You’re not paying into someone’s pocket — you’re buying into something you co-own.

Two Rules That Keep It Honest

  • Full transparency. Everyone can see the total in the kitty at any time. Every dollar in and out is logged.
  • The group decides everything. No single person controls the money. Spending happens by vote.

How We Make Money

A member finds a car they can buy and flip. They request funds from the club and lay out the plan. The group votes. If it passes, the club funds it — and shares the profit.

Joining & Leaving

  • Leave on your own (moving, life happens): get 100% of what you put in back. No profit share.
  • Voted out by the group: get 50% of what you put in. The majority spoke — your exit is in shame. The rest stays with the members who stayed.

How We Vote

Any member can request funds. They post the amount, what it’s for, the expected return, and who’s doing the work. Everyone votes instantly. If the group trusts the idea and the profit’s there, it passes. We’ll run it all through a Discord server so it’s fast, fair, and on the record.

It’s all about growing the club’s funds — together.

The agreement every member reads and signs before joining.

SECTION 01

Mission

Carnalitos is a member-owned car club and investment fund. Members pool money each month and put it to work — flipping cars, selling food and drinks at shows, and other group-approved ventures — to grow a shared pot that benefits everyone. Decisions are made by the group, and the books are open to all members at all times.

SECTION 02

Membership

  • Monthly contribution: $50 per member, due on the same date each month.
  • Tracking: each member’s total contributions are tracked individually and visible to all.
  • New members: admitted by group vote. A new member’s profit share begins only from their join date forward.
  • Missed payments: miss two months in a row and you lose voting rights until caught up; the group may vote to remove you.
SECTION 03

The Money: Two Pools

To keep exits and payouts fair, the club separates money into two pools:

  • Contributions — the money each member personally paid in.
  • Profits — everything the club earned on top through investments and sales.

The kitty balance (both pools combined) is visible to every member at all times.

SECTION 04

Leaving the Club

  • A member who leaves on their own gets back everything they put in, but forfeits any share of profits not yet paid out.
  • A member voted out keeps only 50% of their contributions. The forfeited 50% remains in the club for the members who stayed. This is the cost of an exit by majority vote.
  • Money already paid out in past profit splits is theirs to keep and is never clawed back.
SECTION 05

Investments & Fund Requests

Any member may request funds from the club to make money for the club. Every request must include:

  1. Amount requested (e.g., $2,400).
  2. Purpose — exactly what the money buys.
  3. Expected return — what gets returned to the club and the projected profit.
  4. Timeline — when the funds and profit come back.
  5. Who does the work and what happens if the plan fails.

Worked Example

A member requests $2,400 to buy and fix up a car, planning to sell it for $5,000 and return $4,000 to the club. The club nets $1,600 profit. With 8 members, that’s $200 each.

Risk Rules

  • Skin in the game: the requesting member contributes a portion of the buy (suggested 20%); the club funds the rest. Losses are shared in the same ratio. This keeps requests honest.
  • Labor cut (optional, group decides): the member doing the hands-on work may take an agreed cut of the profit off the top before the even split, since flipping is real work.
  • Concentration cap: no single investment may use more than 40% of the kitty. We never bet the whole pot on one deal.
SECTION 06

Club Events & Sales

The same model funds our shows. The club uses its own funds to buy food, drinks, and supplies, sells them at events, and the profit flows back into the pot. Any member can propose an event purchase using the same fund-request process.

SECTION 07

Voting

  • Where: all proposals and votes happen in the club Discord server so everything is fast and on the record.
  • Spending money: requires a two-thirds (2/3) majority of voting members to pass.
  • Removing a member or changing the charter: also requires a two-thirds majority.
  • Quorum: at least 75% of members must cast a vote for the result to count.
  • Time limit: proposals stay open for 48 hours, then voting closes.
  • Open floor: any member can bring a suggestion. The group decides everything as a whole.
SECTION 08

Money Handling & Trust

  • Where it’s held: club funds sit in a shared/transparent account, not in any one person’s pocket.
  • Open books: every deposit, withdrawal, and balance is logged and viewable by all members at any time.
  • Roles: where possible, the person who holds the money (treasurer) is separate from the person who leads, so no one controls both.
SECTION 09

Profit Splits

  • When: the group votes on when to distribute profits versus reinvest them to keep growing the pot.
  • How: the default is an even split among active members. The group may choose to weight splits by how long or how much each member has contributed, decided by vote.
SECTION 10

Legal & Tax Note

Pooling money to invest and sharing profits can create tax and legal obligations, and the specifics vary by state. Profits may count as taxable income, and the group should consider a written operating agreement or an LLC to protect everyone. Before the club holds significant money, the group should get a one-time consultation with a small-business attorney or accountant. This charter is a member agreement, not legal or financial advice.

SECTION 11

Amendments

This charter can be changed by a two-thirds vote of members. Any approved change is recorded with the date and applies going forward.
